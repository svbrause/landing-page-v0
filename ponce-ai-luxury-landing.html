Quick answers

Quick answers

What does the medical assistant actually do?

Takes front and side photos of the patient, collects treatment interests and history, and runs a quick skin quiz. About 10 minutes during check-in — everything feeds into Ponce automatically before the provider walks in.

How long does it take to build the treatment plan?

Most providers complete the plan in 5–10 minutes. Ponce organizes photos, quiz results, and interests in one view. The provider adds recommendations and sends the post-visit summary in a few taps.

What's in the post-visit summary?

Annotated photos of the patient's face, personalized treatment recommendations with explanations, provider videos about each treatment, before/after examples from patients with similar concerns, and a booking link for next steps. Patients can access it anytime from their phone.

Does the patient need to download an app?

No app to download. The post-visit summary opens in any mobile browser — just like a link. Patients can save it to their home screen if they want easy access later.

Do I need any hardware or equipment?

None. Photos are taken in your clinic on any smartphone or tablet. The provider uses Ponce in a browser on any device. No cameras, no devices, no IT support.

Will this feel pushy to my patients?

The opposite. Patients receive a personalized plan they explore at their own pace — with explanations and visuals instead of a sales pitch. That's why patients rate the experience 4.5 out of 5.

What does it cost?

Monthly software subscription — a fraction of what hardware systems cost. Most practices see it pay for itself within the first month from additional treatments booked. We'll walk through the exact math on your demo call.

Can I try it before I commit?

Yes. Every new practice starts with a free pilot: 5 patients, 30 days, zero cost. You see real numbers from your own practice before making any decision.
